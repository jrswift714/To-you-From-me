Come and See

How insidious, this loneliness is

By way of great terror should we look to the sky and see 5 fingers of darkness blotting out our once loved comrade that lit the land,

Leaving a dark fog to confuse and perpetuate our fumbling meander to grasp one another.

We mourn what we once had, the notion of what was lost to us drives our misery,

Into deeper, darker pathways of winding torture.

We look to the sky, and know it’s looking back, there to smirk at our wallowing and drink in the horror of our narrow experience.

Some say it is our quintessential flaw, that we should look for hope when stark truth hovers in the ebony expanse of our once-great gods.

‘Where are they?’ We’ve asked, ‘where are you?’

We’ve scorned the deities long ago, the nail hammered to dust long ago, more pondering is useless since this long ago.

As is the rest of it.

In truth, there is no known ‘we’,

In my rolling stumble across this desolate tundra, I am alone, but that cannot be possible, because I know that we did not all die.

We could not have, for then what would the sky drink?

So quell my panic with a slapdash argument that there must be others, and so we are a ‘we’ and not my only ‘I’.

But maybe, I am I and we is dead, and so I could banish this sky by seeking to shred

My fragile neck on the stark rocks that pockmark the shocks

Of gray torture that is sure

To not be here when I am gone

Because I am all it has. And when I am gone, then it will starve.

How insidious the sky must be. I am glad to bid it farewell.
